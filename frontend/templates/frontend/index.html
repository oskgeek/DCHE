Strategies for Addressing Issues & Problems:

Pakistan has committed to implement the IHR (2005) as per article 5, Claus (1) till 2015. So within 2 year short time period we have to adopt following remedial measures:

  • Establish well equipped health care facilities with provision of sufficient train staff and diagnosis facilities, at all entry and exit points like at international Airports, Sea ports and Health Check Post at ground crossing for prompt assessment and care of ill travelers.
  • Ensure the availability of ample premises to provide preventive health care services at each entry and exit points.
  • Provision of well equipped transportation/Ambulance facility at each entry and exit point to shift the ill traveler to tertiary hospital.
  • Establish isolation centers/quarantine hospitals at each entry and exit points or at least one in each province and region.
  • Up-gradation and restructuring of the Directorate of Central Health Establishments.

To accomplish the above task the government of Pakistan may ask to WHO and other donor agencies for necessary assistance and support.

The purpose and scope of IHR (2005) are to prevent, protect against, control and provide a public health response to the international spread of disease in ways that are commensurate with and restricted to public health risk and which avoid unnecessary interference with international traffic & trade.
